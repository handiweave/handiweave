Crafts of Himachal

विरासत-ए-हिमाचल

Original Documentation by Handiweave

Introduction: Himachal Pradesh, known for its rich cultural heritage and traditional skills, is home to a wide variety of indigenous crafts. These crafts are deeply rooted in the geography, climate, and history of each region. This document provides a comprehensive district-wise list of crafts categorized by material type (fiber, metal, wood, soil/clay, bamboo, leather, grass, stone, etc.), along with the traditional techniques and current status.

Districts

1. Chamba (चंबा) 2. Kangra (कांगड़ा) 3. Kullu (कुल्लू) 4. Lahaul & Spiti 5. Mandi (मंडी) 6. Kinnaur (किन्नौर) 7. Hamirpur 8. Una (ऊना) 9. Bilaspur 10. Solan (सोलन) 11. Sirmour 12. Shimla

1. District Chamba

जिला चंबा

A. Chamba Rumal | चंबा रुमाल

Fibre & Embroidery (GI Tagged)

The Chamba Rumal is a traditional embroidered handkerchief or square cloth, originating from Chamba district. It is an exquisite art form known for its double-sided embroidery that depicts stories from Indian epics like the Ramayana and Mahabharata.
यह एक वर्गाकार कपड़ा होता है, जिस पर रामायण, महाभारत और अन्य पौराणिक कथाओं के दृश्यों की दोहरी कढ़ाई की जाती है।

Materials Used | सामग्री
  • Fine muslin or khaddar (cotton/silk fabric).
  • Silk threads (often untwisted).
  • Natural or vegetable dyes (traditionally used).
Process of Making | निर्माण प्रक्रिया
  • Fabric Preparation: The muslin is stretched over a frame.
  • Design Tracing: Mythological scenes are hand-drawn using charcoal.
  • Embroidery: Executed using the double satin stitch (do-rukha tanka), ensuring both sides mirror the same design.
Present Status

Revival efforts by NGOs and the Himachal government have brought Chamba Rumal back to prominence. It is now GI-tagged.

B. Chamba Leather | चंबा चप्पल

Leather Craft

Famous for the handcrafted Chamba Chappal. Known for their real leather base, intricate hand embroidery.
ये चप्पलें असली चमड़े से बनती हैं, जिन पर सुंदर हस्तनिर्मित कढ़ाई की जाती है।

Materials

Vegetable-tanned real leather. Brass buttons, beads.

Status

Nearing extinction due to synthetic footwear.

C. Metal Craft & Jewellery | धातु शिल्प

Renowned for Chamba Thaal (plates) and temple bells. Craftsmen use copper, brass, silver.
तांबा, पीतल, चांदी और सोने से निर्मित ये शिल्प मंदिरों से जुड़े हैं।

D. Traditional Jewellery | पारंपरिक आभूषण

Exquisite ornaments like Chandbalis, Chokers, and Pahari hairpins.
पारंपरिक चांदी और सोने के आभूषण।

E. Chamba Painting | चंबा चित्रकला

Pahari Miniature

Characterized by romantic expressions and fine brushwork.
बारीक रेखाएँ, हल्के रंग और भावनात्मक गहराई।

Folk Instruments

F. Traditional Musical Instruments | पारंपरिक वाद्य यंत्र

Nagara, Dhol, Shehnai, Ransinga. Handcrafted from wood, copper, and hide. Used in fairs and rituals.

Wood Carving

G. Chamba Wooden Craft | लकड़ी शिल्प

Wood Carving

Carved temple façades, windows, and 'Sandook' (chests). The forests of Chamba offer abundant high-quality cedar (Deodar).
मंदिरों की दीवारों, खंभों से लेकर घरेलू सजावटी वस्तुओं तक, यह शिल्प चंबा की पहचान है।

Basketry

H. Basketry | चंबा की टोकरियाँ

Grass / Bamboo

Kilta (conical basket) and Tokri woven from Ringal bamboo and wild grass. Used for storage and transportation.
चंबा की टोकरियाँ वहाँ के ग्रामीण जीवन का एक अविभाज्य हिस्सा हैं।

2. District Kangra

जिला कांगड़ा

1. Gaddi Pattu | गद्दी पट्टू

Wool

Worn traditionally by the Gaddi tribal community, woven on pit looms.
गद्दी समुदाय द्वारा पहना जाने वाला ऊनी वस्त्र।

2. Kangra Painting | कांगड़ा चित्रकला

Famed for delicate lines, soft colors, and "Emotions in eyes". Themes revolve around Radha-Krishna.

3. Basohli Painting | बसोहली चित्रकला

Early Pahari Style

Precursor to Kangra style. Known for bold primary colors, large eyes, and vigorous lines.
कांगड़ा शैली से पहले की शैली, जो अपने तेज़ रंगों के लिए जानी जाती है।

4. Slate Craft | स्लेट शिल्प

Mined in Dehra/Shahpur. Used for roofing and blackboards.

Appliqué Art

5. Drass Dub Ma | ड्रास डुब मा

Appliqué Thangka

Tibetan Buddhist appliqué Thangka popular in Dharamshala. Stitched using silk and brocade pieces (not painted). Used in monasteries.

Metal Embossing

6. Thakkat Ka Kaam | ठक्कट का काम

Sheet Metal Work

Embossing motifs on copper/brass sheets for temples (doors, masks, bells).
यह शिल्प मुख्यतः तांबे या पीतल की पतली चादरों पर आकृतियाँ उकेरने का है।

7. Thangka Painting | थांका चित्रकला

Painting on Cloth

Sacred Tibetan Buddhist painting depicting deities/mandalas. Painted on cotton/silk canvas using natural mineral pigments.
थांका एक तिब्बती बौद्ध धार्मिक चित्रकला है।

Dharamshala Wood

8. Woodwork of Dharamshala | धर्मशाला की लकड़ी शिल्प

Mix of Himachali and Tibetan influence. Used for altar tables (chokste), carved windows, and prayer wheels.

9. Clay Craft of Kangra | कांगड़ा की मिट्टी शिल्प

Potters (Kumhars) create Ghara, Kulhad, Diyas, and decorative toys. Uses 'Lohar mitti'.

10. Bahloti & Charoti Metal | बहलोटी धातु शिल्प

Traditional casting of bells (Ghanta), ritual lamps (Deepdan), and vessels using lost-wax technique.

Rope Making

11. Traditional Rope-Making | रस्सी निर्माण

Made from 'Beul' tree bark fibers and paddy husk. Used for tying cattle and charpai weaving.

12. Bamboo & Basketry | बांस शिल्प

Abundant bamboo in Kangra is used to make Tokri, Kilta, and furniture.

3. District Kullu

जिला कुल्लू

1. Wool Weaving | कुल्लू शॉल

Handloom (GI Tagged)

Famous for geometric borders (Akhar, Chakram) and vibrant colors. Includes Shawls and Pattus.

2. Hand Knitting | हाथ बुनाई

Sweaters, mufflers, socks, caps. Mainly by women in winters. Common in Sainj, Banjar.

3. Malana Cap Embroidery | मलाणा टोपी

Embroidery

Caps made from handwoven woolen fabric, embroidered with colored threads in zigzag designs. Symbol of Malana identity.

4. Pullan | पुल्लन

Footwear

Wool-felted grass slippers. Made from sheep wool and dried grass (bhojpatra/bhabhar). Snow-resistant.
अब यह शिल्प लुप्तप्राय है, केवल धार्मिक समारोहों के लिए बनाया जाता है।

Grass Mat

5. Mandri | मंडड़ी

Grass Mat Weaving using Munj grass. Used for sitting or drying grains. Mostly extinct.

6. Woodcraft (Banauta) | काष्ठ कला

Architecture

Includes 'Kath-Kuni' style temples, carved door frames, and 'Dev Manji' (Palanquins) for deities.
काष्ठ-कुणी शैली के मंदिर और देवताओं की पालकियों पर की गई नक्काशी।

7. Ritual Metal Craft | मोहरा और वाद्य यंत्र

Creation of 'Mohras' (Metal masks of deities) in silver/brass. Production of ritual trumpets like Karnal and Narsingha.

8. Jewellery Making | आभूषण शिल्प

Worn by Gaddi and Pahari women. Items: Paunchi, Chandanhaar, Toras, Gokhru.

9. Stone Craft | पत्थर शिल्प

Seen in ancient stone temples of Naggar and Bajaura. Carving on grey schist and slate.

10. Sela (Goat Hair Coat) | सेला

Tribal Winter Wear

Thick overcoats, Socks and sheet made of goat hair twisted by hands, used by shepherds/locals in upper Kullu (Malana, Banjar,Lug Valley). Woven manually from goat hair.
यह गद्दी और पशुपालकों के लिए सर्दियों में जीवन रक्षक वस्त्र माना जाता है। अब यह शिल्प विलुप्त होने की कगार पर है।

4. District Lahaul & Spiti

जिला लाहौल-स्पीति

1. Wool Weaving (Socks) | लाहौली मोजे

GI Tagged

Hand-knitted socks and gloves using indigenous geometric patterns. Made from local sheep and yak wool. Products: Pattu, Cholu, Thudung.

2. Kharkhana (Carpet Weaving) | खरखाना

Tibetan Knotting

Vertical loom weaving of carpets and rugs. Motifs include dragons, floral scrolls, and Buddhist symbols. Colors: Vegetable dyes.

3. Thangka & Appliqué Art | थंका चित्रकला

Religious Art

Religious scrolls depicting Buddha and Mandalas. Preserved in monasteries like Tabo and Ki.

Monastic Woodwork

4. Woodwork & Carving | लकड़ी का काम

Monastic furniture, altar tables, carved window frames. Motifs: Dragons, lotuses. Preserved in Tabo and Dhankar monasteries.

Religious Metal

5. Religious Metal Craft | धार्मिक धातु शिल्प

Buddhist ritual items: Butter lamps, prayer wheels, trumpets. Made of copper, bronze, silver using lost-wax casting.

6. Clay & Mud Crafts | मिट्टी शिल्प

Monastic Craft

Large clay idols inside monasteries (Tabo) made using natural mud/straw. Also includes 'Torma' (Butter sculptures) for rituals.

Bone Craft

7. Bone & Horn Craft | अस्थि शिल्प

Traditional jewelry made from yak horn and bone. Items: Beads, amulet cases.

Endangered

8. Endangered Crafts | लुप्तप्राय शिल्प

Leather Boot Making (Spiti Boots), Wool Rope Making (Namlo), Painted Scroll Manuscripts.

5. District Mandi

जिला मंडी

Clay & Stone Craft | मंडी के शिल्प

Clay: Fermentation jars and pottery of Gohar.
Stone: Idols of 'Chhoti Kashi'.
Weaving: Shawls and Pattus in Seraj Valley.

Temple Architecture & Metal | मंदिर वास्तुकला

Intricate woodwork in Devta homes and Metal bells/vessels in Rewalsar.

6. District Kinnaur

जिला किन्नौर

1. Kinnauri Weaving | किन्नौरी शॉल

Textile

Handwoven Shawls, Dhoru (cloak), and Caps. Famous for elaborate geometric borders involving religious symbols.

2. Ritual Ropes | देव रस्सियाँ

Sacred Craft

Braided from Beul bark, yak hair, or hemp. Used to tie Deity Palanquins during Fulaich festival. Symbolize strength and divine connection.

3. Silver Jewellery | चांदी के आभूषण

Heavy silver jewellery with oxidized finish. Items: Chakker (headband), Bulak (nose ring), Digra (shawl pin).

7. District Hamirpur

Bamboo & Iron | हमीरपुर के शिल्प

Bamboo/Grass: Daari (mats) and Tokri.
Ironware: Traditional rural blacksmithing tools.

Wood & Knitting | लकड़ी और बुनाई

Agricultural tools, toys, and hand-knitted woolens.

8. District Una

Khajur Mats & Rope | खजूर की चटाई

Khajur Mats: Woven from dried Date Palm leaves. Naturally cool and eco-friendly.
Rope Making: From 'Beul' tree bark and paddy husk.

9. District Bilaspur

Bilaspur Heritage | बिलासपुर के शिल्प

Pattu: Handwoven with simple geometric designs.
Pottery: Black and red soil pottery of Ghumarwin.
Charpai: Rope weaving using natural fibers.

10. District Solan

Pine Needle Craft | चीड़ सुई शिल्प

Eco Craft

An emerging sustainable craft in Dharampur/Kasauli involving weaving fallen pine needles into baskets and trays. Promotes zero-waste design.

11. District Sirmour

Sirmour Traditions | सिरमौर के शिल्प

Ringal Craft: Fine basketry and 'Chhatries' (Rain shields).
Endangered: 'Sela' (Goat Hair Coat) & 'Chharpat' (Grass Raincoat).

12. District Shimla

Shimla Heritage Crafts | शिमला के शिल्प

  • Bushahri Topi: Traditional cap with velvet band.
  • Wood Carving: Temple architecture in Rohru and Jubbal.
  • Doll Making: Traditional cloth dolls.
  • Fruit Box: Semi-industrial wooden crates.
