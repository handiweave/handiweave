Crafts of Himachal

विरासत-ए-हिमाचल

Original Documentation by Handiweave

Introduction: Himachal Pradesh, known for its rich cultural heritage and traditional skills, is home to a wide variety of indigenous crafts. These crafts are deeply rooted in the geography, climate, and history of each region. This document provides a comprehensive district-wise list of crafts categorized by material type (fiber, metal, wood, soil/clay, bamboo, leather, grass, stone, etc.), along with the traditional techniques and current status (active, endangered, or extinct).

Districts

1. Chamba (चंबा) 2. Kangra (कांगड़ा) 3. Kullu (कुल्लू) 4. Lahaul & Spiti 5. Mandi (मंडी) 6. Kinnaur (किन्नौर) 7. Hamirpur 8. Una (ऊना) 9. Bilaspur 10. Solan (सोलन) 11. Sirmour 12. Shimla

1. District Chamba

जिला चंबा

A. Chamba Rumal | चंबा रुमाल

Fibre & Embroidery (GI Tagged)

The Chamba Rumal is a traditional embroidered handkerchief or square cloth, originating from Chamba district in Himachal Pradesh. It is an exquisite art form known for its double-sided embroidery that depicts stories from Indian epics like the Ramayana and Mahabharata.
चंबा रुमाल हिमाचल प्रदेश के चंबा ज़िले की पारंपरिक कढ़ाई की कला है। यह एक वर्गाकार कपड़ा होता है, जिस पर रामायण, महाभारत और अन्य पौराणिक कथाओं के दृश्यों की दोहरी कढ़ाई की जाती है।

Materials Used | सामग्री
  • Fine muslin or khaddar (cotton/silk fabric) / महीन मलमल या खद्दर
  • Silk threads (often untwisted) / रेशमी धागे
  • Natural or vegetable dyes (traditionally used) / पारंपरिक रूप से प्राकृतिक रंग
Process of Making | निर्माण प्रक्रिया
  • Fabric Preparation: The muslin or cotton cloth is stretched over a frame.
  • Design Tracing: Mythological scenes are hand-drawn using charcoal or stencil methods.
  • Embroidery: Executed using the double satin stitch (do-rukha tanka), which ensures that both sides of the rumal mirror the same design. Stitches are closely packed for detailed narrative visuals.
  • Finishing: Edges are hemmed neatly, and the rumal is pressed and sometimes framed.
Themes & Motifs | विषयवस्तु

Episodes from Ramayana, Mahabharata, Krishna Leela. Royal court scenes, marriage processions. Nature elements like flowers, birds, trees.

Present Status

Although the craft saw decline post-Independence, revival efforts by NGOs, the Himachal government, and the DC Handicrafts have brought Chamba Rumal back to prominence. It is now GI-tagged.

B. Chamba Leather | चंबा चप्पल

Leather Craft

The Chamba Leather Craft, particularly famous for the handcrafted Chamba Chappal, is a unique artisanal tradition. These slippers are known for their real leather base, intricate hand embroidery, and use during local festivals.
चंबा चमड़ा शिल्प, विशेष रूप से चंबा चप्पल... ये चप्पलें असली चमड़े से बनती हैं, जिन पर सुंदर हस्तनिर्मित कढ़ाई की जाती है।

Materials Used
  • Vegetable-tanned real leather (goat or buffalo hide).
  • Cotton or woolen embroidery threads. Brass buttons, mirrors, beads.
Process of Making | निर्माण प्रक्रिया
  • Leather Tanning: Raw hides are cleaned using traditional methods with bark and walnut husk.
  • Cutting & Shaping: Leather is cut into shapes for the sole and upper covering using handmade templates.
  • Hand Embroidery: Upper leather parts are hand-embroidered with bright threads in floral or geometric motifs.
  • Stitching: The embroidered upper is stitched to the sole using strong leather threads.
Design Features

Handmade, eco-friendly, breathable. Vibrant floral or geometric embroidery. Closed front, open-back structure.

Current Status

Due to the rise of synthetic footwear, this traditional craft is nearing extinction. Revival efforts under schemes like NHDP are helping.

C. Metal Craft & Jewellery | धातु शिल्प और आभूषण

Metal

Chamba is renowned for its traditional metal crafts and ornate jewellery made using copper, brass, silver, and gold. Items like Chamba Thaal (metal plates) and temple bells reflect skilled craftsmanship.
तांबा, पीतल, चांदी और सोने से निर्मित ये शिल्प मंदिरों, राजघरानों और धार्मिक आयोजनों से गहराई से जुड़े हैं।

Major Products
  • Chamba Thaal (decorative copper/brass plates).
  • Temple Bells, Kalash, Puja Thalis.
  • Silver & Gold Jewellery (chandbalis, toe rings, chokers). Pahari-style Hairpins.
Process of Making

Metal Craft: Selection & Melting -> Casting/Shaping (using dies/molds) -> Etching & Engraving (Hand tools used for floral patterns) -> Polishing.

Jewellery: Design Sketching -> Metal Preparation -> Filigree Work & Setting (Tiny beads/stones) -> Final Polish.

Status

Many master artisans have retired. However, government exhibitions and GI registration efforts are trying to revive this heritage.

D. Traditional Musical Instruments | पारंपरिक वाद्य यंत्र

Wood / Metal

Chamba district is home to several traditional musical instruments used in religious rituals, local fairs (mela), and folk performances like Kariyala.
ये वाद्य यंत्र मुख्यतः लकड़ी, धातु, जानवरों की खाल और बाँस से हस्तनिर्मित होते हैं।

Major Instruments
  • Nagara (नगाड़ा): Large two-sided drum used during temple processions.
  • Dhol (ढोल): Barrel-shaped drum played with sticks.
  • Shehnai (शहनाई): Wind instrument of wood and brass.
  • Ransinga (रनसिंगा): Curved trumpet made of copper or brass.
  • Karnal: Straight long trumpet.
  • Chimta: Metal tongs with bells.
Making Process

Body Construction (carving wood) -> Drum Head Attachment (Animal skin stretched) -> Tuning (Checking sound) -> Decoration (Painting/Engraving).

Status

Declining due to machine-made alternatives. Only a handful of families continue this craft.

E. Chamba Wooden Craft | लकड़ी शिल्प

Wood Carving

The forests of Chamba offer abundant high-quality cedar (Deodar) and pine. The craft includes intricately carved temple façades, windows, pillars, and decorative wooden boxes.
मंदिरों की दीवारों, खंभों, दरवाजों से लेकर घरेलू सजावटी वस्तुओं तक, यह शिल्प चंबा की सांस्कृतिक पहचान का प्रतीक है।

Major Products
  • Carved temple doors & pillars.
  • Jharokhas (windows) with floral/mythological carvings.
  • Wooden chests & jewellery boxes (Sandook).
  • Wooden masks for folk theatre.
Materials

Deodar (Cedrus deodara), Pine, Natural dyes.

Status

Fading due to modern construction. Seen mostly in temples and heritage homes.

F. Chamba Basketry | चंबा की टोकरियाँ

Grass / Bamboo

Crafted using local bamboo (baans), ringal, and wild grasses. These handwoven products are used for storage and transportation.
चंबा की टोकरियाँ वहाँ के ग्रामीण जीवन का एक अविभाज्य हिस्सा हैं। ये स्थानीय बाँस (रिंगाल) और वन घासों से बनाई जाती हैं।

Products
  • Tokri: Circular baskets for vegetables.
  • Kilta: Conical basket carried on the back.
  • Mandri: Flat grass mat.
  • Chhaj: Winnowing tray.
Process

Harvesting -> Splitting Bamboo -> Weaving (Interlacing techniques) -> Finishing.

Status

Plastic containers have reduced demand, but eco-tourism is bringing renewed interest.

G. Chamba Painting | चंबा चित्रकला

Pahari Miniature

Distinctive school of Pahari miniature painting (17th-19th Century). Characterized by fine brushwork, intricate detailing, and emotional expressions.
बारीक रेखाएँ, हल्के रंग और भावनात्मक गहराई इस शैली की पहचान हैं।

Process
  • Paper: Handmade paper polished with shells.
  • Sketching: Outlines drawn with squirrel-hair brushes.
  • Coloring: Natural dyes (Vermilion, Harad, Lapis Lazuli, Gold leaf).
  • Burnishing: Polished with agate.
Themes

Radha-Krishna, Ramayana, Court Life, Nature.

2. District Kangra

जिला कांगड़ा

1. Gaddi Pattu | गद्दी पट्टू

Wool

Worn traditionally by the Gaddi tribal community, this woolen fabric is handwoven on pit looms in Kangra valley.
गद्दी समुदाय द्वारा पहना जाने वाला, यह ऊनी वस्त्र पिट लूम पर बुना जाता है।

Process

Wool is locally shorn, cleaned, spun, and dyed in natural colors. Woven into shawls, pattus, and blankets. Often embellished with embroidery.

2. Kangra Painting | कांगड़ा चित्रकला

Miniature Art

Evolved from Basohli School, famed for delicate lines, soft colors, and "Emotions in eyes". Themes revolve around Shringar-Rasa and Bhakti. Patronized by Raja Sansar Chand.

Materials

Natural pigments from stones/flowers. Fine squirrel-hair brushes. Handmade paper.

Signature Feature

“Emotions in eyes”—Kangra paintings are unmatched in their expression.

3. Slate Craft | स्लेट शिल्प

Stone

Mined in Dehra/Shahpur. Used for roofing and blackboards. Artisans craft engraved nameplates and ritual slabs.
स्लेट पत्थर की नक्काशी और छत निर्माण।

4. Drass Dub Ma | ड्रास डुब मा

Appliqué Thangka

Tibetan Buddhist appliqué Thangka popular in Dharamshala. Stitched using silk and brocade pieces (not painted). Used in monasteries.

5. Knitting / Wool Craft | ऊन बुनाई

Region: Palampur, Baijnath. Women engage in knitting socks, sweaters, caps with traditional motifs. Promoted by SHGs.

6. Thakkat Ka Kaam | ठक्कट का काम

Sheet Metal Work

Embossing motifs on copper/brass sheets for temples (doors, masks, bells).
यह शिल्प मुख्यतः तांबे या पीतल की पतली चादरों पर आकृतियाँ उकेरने का है।

Sheet Selection -> Tracing Design -> Embossing/Engraving (with chisels/hammers) -> Polishing.

7. Thangka Painting | थांका चित्रकला

Painting on Cloth

Sacred Tibetan Buddhist painting depicting deities/mandalas. Painted on cotton/silk canvas using natural mineral pigments.
थांका एक तिब्बती बौद्ध धार्मिक चित्रकला है।

Process: Canvas preparation (glue/chalk) -> Sketching -> Color Filling (Malachite/Lapis) -> Gold Work -> Mounting (Brocade).

8. Woodwork of Dharamshala | धर्मशाला की लकड़ी शिल्प

Mix of Himachali and Tibetan influence. Used for altar tables (chokste), carved windows, and prayer wheels. Materials: Deodar, Walnut.

9. Clay Craft of Kangra | कांगड़ा की मिट्टी शिल्प

Pottery

Potters (Kumhars) create Ghara, Kulhad, Diyas, and decorative toys. Uses 'Lohar mitti'.
कांगड़ा की यह प्राचीन शिल्प परंपरा सदियों से घरेलू और धार्मिक उपयोगों के लिए विकसित हुई है।

Products

Ghara, Surahi, Diya, Nandi figurine, Clay elephants.

Status

Declining due to plastic/steel, but demand for eco-friendly kulhads is reviving it.

10. Bahloti & Charoti Metal | बहलोटी धातु शिल्प

Bell Metal

Traditional casting of bells (Ghanta), ritual lamps (Deepdan), and vessels using lost-wax technique. Specific to Nagrota Bagwan belt.

Process

Metal Mixing (Bronze/Brass) -> Wax Molding -> Clay Coating -> Firing -> Finishing.

Status

Endangered. Only a few families remain.

11. Traditional Rope-Making | रस्सी निर्माण

Made from 'Beul' tree bark fibers and paddy husk. Used for tying cattle and charpai weaving. Local Name: Rassi/Baandhi.

12. Pine Needle Craft | चीड़ सुई शिल्प

Eco Craft

Coiling fallen pine needles (pirul) into baskets, trays, and mats. Eco-friendly and helps prevent forest fires.
यह उत्पाद बायो-डिग्रेडेबल और टिकाऊ होते हैं।

3. District Kullu

जिला कुल्लू

1. Wool Weaving | कुल्लू शॉल

Handloom (GI Tagged)

Includes Kullu Shawl, Pattu, and Lohi. Woven on fly-shuttle pit looms using Angora, Merino, and local wool. Famous for geometric borders.

Carding -> Spinning -> Dyeing -> Warping -> Weaving. Embellished with motifs like Akhar, Chakram.

2. Hand Knitting | हाथ बुनाई

Sweaters, mufflers, socks, caps. Mainly by women in winters. Common in Sainj, Banjar.

3. Malana Cap Embroidery | मलाणा टोपी

Caps made from handwoven woolen fabric, embroidered with colored threads in zigzag designs. Symbol of Malana identity.

4. Pullan | पुल्लन

Footwear

Wool-felted grass slippers. Made from sheep wool and dried grass (bhojpatra/bhabhar). Snow-resistant.
अब यह शिल्प लुप्तप्राय है, केवल धार्मिक समारोहों के लिए बनाया जाता है।

5. Mandri | मंडड़ी

Grass Mat Weaving using Munj grass. Used for sitting or drying grains. Mostly extinct.

6. Woodcraft (Banauta) | काष्ठ कला

Architecture

Includes 'Kath-Kuni' style temples, carved door frames, and 'Dev Manji' (Palanquins) for deities. Deodar wood is primarily used.
काष्ठ-कुणी शैली के मंदिर और देवताओं की पालकियों पर की गई नक्काशी।

7. Ritual Metal Craft | मोहरा और वाद्य यंत्र

Thathera Craft

Creation of 'Mohras' (Metal masks of deities) in silver/brass. Production of ritual trumpets like Karnal and Narsingha. Made by Thatheras using hammering and casting.

Items
  • Devta's crown, earrings, Khanda (sword).
  • Bronze Idol Making (Lost Wax Process).
  • Sheet Metal Work (Thakkat ka kaam).

8. Jewellery Making | आभूषण शिल्प

Worn by Gaddi and Pahari women. Items: Paunchi, Chandanhaar, Toras, Gokhru.

9. Stone Craft | पत्थर शिल्प

Seen in ancient stone temples of Naggar and Bajaura. Carving on grey schist and slate.

10. Sela (Goat Hair Coat) | सेला

Tribal Winter Wear

Thick overcoats used by shepherds in upper Kullu (Malana, Banjar). Woven manually from goat hair.
यह गद्दी और पशुपालकों के लिए सर्दियों में जीवन रक्षक वस्त्र माना जाता है। अब यह शिल्प विलुप्त होने की कगार पर है।

4. District Lahaul & Spiti

जिला लाहौल-स्पीति

1. Wool Weaving (Socks) | लाहौली मोजे

GI Tagged

Hand-knitted socks and gloves using indigenous geometric patterns. Made from local sheep and yak wool. Products: Pattu, Cholu, Thudung.

2. Kharkhana (Carpet Weaving) | खरखाना

Tibetan Knotting

Vertical loom weaving of carpets and rugs. Motifs include dragons, floral scrolls, and Buddhist symbols. Colors: Vegetable dyes.

3. Thangka & Appliqué Art | थंका चित्रकला

Religious Art

Religious scrolls depicting Buddha and Mandalas. Painted on cotton canvas or stitched using silk/brocade (Appliqué).

4. Woodwork & Carving | लकड़ी का काम

Monastic furniture, altar tables, carved window frames. Motifs: Dragons, lotuses. Preserved in Tabo and Dhankar monasteries.

5. Religious Metal Craft | धार्मिक धातु शिल्प

Buddhist ritual items: Butter lamps, prayer wheels, trumpets. Made of copper, bronze, silver using lost-wax casting.

6. Clay & Mud Crafts | मिट्टी शिल्प

Large clay idols inside monasteries (Tabo) made using natural mud/straw. Also includes 'Torma' (Butter sculptures) for rituals.

7. Bone & Horn Craft | अस्थि शिल्प

Traditional jewelry made from yak horn and bone. Items: Beads, amulet cases.

8. Endangered Crafts | लुप्तप्राय शिल्प

Leather Boot Making (Spiti Boots), Wool Rope Making (Namlo), Painted Scroll Manuscripts.

5. District Mandi

जिला मंडी

Mandi Heritage Crafts | मंडी के शिल्प

  • Wool Weaving: Woolen Pattus and Shawls in Seraj and Balh Valley.
  • Bamboo Craft: Tokri and mats in Karsog.
  • Wood Carving: Temple architecture (Devta homes).
  • Stone Carving: Idols of 'Chhoti Kashi'.
  • Clay Pottery: Fermentation jars and pottery of Gohar.
  • Metal Craft: Temple bells and ritual vessels in Rewalsar.
  • Endangered: Dhaniya rope (Dheeli) made from wild grass.

6. District Kinnaur

जिला किन्नौर

1. Kinnauri Weaving | किन्नौरी शॉल

Textile

Handwoven Shawls, Dhoru (cloak), and Caps. Famous for elaborate geometric borders involving religious symbols.
धार्मिक प्रतीकों वाले विस्तृत ज्यामितीय किनारों के लिए प्रसिद्ध।

2. Silver Jewellery | चांदी के आभूषण

Heavy silver jewellery with oxidized finish. Items: Chakker (headband), Bulak (nose ring), Digra (shawl pin).

3. Woodwork | लकड़ी का काम

Kinnauri houses with intricate woodwork. Temple façades with floral/mythological carvings.

4. Basketry & Rope | टोकरा और रस्सी

Baskets for apples made from Willow twigs. Namlo ropes used to carry headloads.

5. Carpets & Namdas | कालीन और नमदा

Sheep wool carpets and felted Namdas. Designs: Stylized flowers, diamonds, Buddhist patterns.

6. Ritual Ropes | देव रस्सियाँ

Sacred Craft

Braided from Beul bark, yak hair, or hemp. Used to tie Deity Palanquins during Fulaich festival. Symbolize strength and divine connection.

7. District Hamirpur

Utility Crafts | हमीरपुर के शिल्प

Wool: Pattu & Handwoven Woolens.
Grass Craft: Daari (mats) and Tokri made from Moonj/Kusha grass.
Wood Craft: Agricultural tools and toys.
Utility Metal: Rural Ironware (Blacksmithing).
Knitting: Hand knitting & Crochet.

8. District Una

Una Traditional Crafts | ऊना के शिल्प

Rope Making: From 'Beul' tree bark and paddy husk.
Clay Craft: Pottery & Diya work in Santoshgarh.
Bamboo: Basketry & Mats.
Khajur Mats: Woven from dried Date Palm leaves. Naturally cool and eco-friendly.
Religious Craft: Jhanjhar, Mohras.

9. District Bilaspur

Bilaspur Heritage | बिलासपुर के शिल्प

Wool: Handwoven Pattu with simple designs.
Bamboo: Tokri, Daari.
Clay: Pottery of Ghumarwin (Black/Red soil).
Charpai: Rope weaving.
Endangered: Grass mats and Beul rope.

10. District Solan

Solan Crafts | सोलन के शिल्प

Bamboo: Basketry in Shivalik ranges.
Pottery: Clay craft of Nalagarh & Arki.
Pine Needle Craft: Sustainable baskets/trays from pine leaves.
Wool: Weaving in Rajgarh belt.
Stone: Temple architecture.

11. District Sirmour

Sirmour Traditions | सिरमौर के शिल्प

Wool: Pattus and coats (Shillai/Rajgarh).
Grass & Ringal: Fine basketry and 'Chhatries' (Rain shields).
Woodcraft: Decorative panels and temples.
Ritual Ropes: For deity accessories.
Metal: Temple lamps and bells.
Endangered: 'Sela' (Goat Hair Coat/socks) & 'Chharpat'.

12. District Shimla

Shimla Heritage Crafts | शिमला के शिल्प

  • Wood Carving: Temple architecture in Rohru/Jubbal.
  • Metal Craft: Brass/Copper work in Sarahan.
  • Handloom: Woolen Pattu/Shawls.
  • Bushahri Topi: Traditional cap with velvet band.
  • Bamboo: Basketry.
  • Doll Making: Traditional cloth dolls.
  • Stone Carving: Temple work.
  • Fruit Box: Semi-industrial wooden crates.
